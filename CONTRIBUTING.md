# Contributing to the Meridian Protocol

Thank you for your interest in contributing. Meridian is an early-stage
Internet-Draft and all constructive input is welcome.

---

## Ways to contribute

### Raise an issue

The simplest way to contribute is to open a GitHub issue. Good issues include:

- Technical concerns about the protocol design
- Questions about the specification text
- Proposals for new features or changes
- Pointers to prior art or related work we should engage with
- Implementation experience (even negative)

Please be specific. "This seems wrong" is less useful than "Section 5.2
requires three geographically distributed vantage points but does not
define what 'geographically distributed' means in practice — here is
why that matters and a suggested clarification."

### Propose a change

If you have a concrete textual change to propose:

1. Fork the repository
2. Make your changes to the `.txt` draft
3. Open a pull request with a clear description of what you changed and why

Note that Internet-Draft plain text format has specific conventions
(72-character line width, specific indentation). Please preserve these.

### Co-authorship

If you have deep expertise in DNS, web archival, digital preservation,
or infrastructure standards and want to be involved as a co-author,
please reach out directly at [trey@propertools.be](mailto:trey@propertools.be)
with a brief note on your background and interest.

---

## What we are particularly looking for

- **DNS expertise** — the MIRROR RR design in Section 5 is the most
  technically ambitious part and most likely to attract pushback. Input
  from people with experience proposing new RR types is especially
  valuable.

- **Web archival expertise** — the relationship between Meridian and
  existing archival infrastructure (Internet Archive, WARC, Heritrix)
  needs careful thought.

- **Implementation experience** — prototype implementations of any
  component (manifest format, delta-sync, MIRROR RR) would be
  enormously valuable for advancing the draft.

- **Mirror operator interest** — organisations willing to operate
  authorised mirrors under the protocol as it matures.

---

## Conduct

This is a technical collaboration. Please be direct, specific, and
constructive. Personal attacks, bad faith engagement, and noise are
unwelcome.
